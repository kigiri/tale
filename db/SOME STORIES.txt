The farmer, his son and their donkey ...
A great story with good moral ...


One fine spring morning, a farmer and his son were taking their donkey to market to sell it.

The father and son were walking and donkey followed them. No sooner had they taken a few steps they met a group of girls walking in the opposite direction.

- "Look at them!", Exclaimed one of the girls, pointing the finger at the farmer,
"They are idiots! They walk on foot when they could get on their ass".

Hearing this, the old man said quietly to his son to ride in the back of the donkey and they went on their way towards the market ...



They passed a group of men sitting on the side of the road and the farmer heard the men say:

"Young people have no respect for their elderly parents today.
Scroll down this lazy boy to rest the legs of his father! ".

The son then went down with a bound and his father took his place ...



Soon, they came across women with their children.

"Look at this cruel man!" Are they cried!
"It works so quickly that the poor boy can hardly keep up!

The farmer is then stopped and lifted the boy to put it behind him ...



They continued their journey and were almost at the market when a merchant stopped them.

"Is it your ass?" Has he asked.

"Yes," replied the farmer.

"Well I am shocked at how you treat it," replied the merchant.
"Two people in the back of a donkey, it is too!
It will surely die this way. Instead, you should wear it! "



Following this advice, the farmer and his son came down from the donkey, tied his legs and carried him. But the donkey refused to be well worn and, struggling to paw, he broke the rope that held his feet and then fell into a river near the road and drowned. The farmer had nothing been done, he returned home empty-handed.

Le fermier ,son fils et leur âne...
Une belle histoire avec une bonne morale...

Un beau matin de printemps, un fermier et son fils emmenaient leur âne au marché pour le vendre.

Le père et son fils marchaient et l'âne les suivait. A peine avaient-ils fait quelques pas qu'ils rencontrèrent un groupe de filles marchant dans la direction opposée.

- " Regardez-les ! ", s'est exclamée une des filles en montrant le fermier du doigt,
"Qu'ils sont idiots ! Ils marchent à pied alors qu'ils pourraient monter sur leur âne! ".

Entendant cela, le vieil homme dit tranquillement à son fils de monter à l'arrière de l'âne puis ils continuèrent leur chemin en direction du marché...

Ils passèrent devant un groupe d'hommes assis sur le côté de la route et le fermier entendit un des hommes dire :

" Les jeunes n'ont plus aucun respect pour leurs vieux parents de nos jours.
Faites descendre ce garçon paresseux pour reposer les jambes de son père! ".

Le fils descendit alors d'un bond et son père prit sa place...

Bientôt, ils croisèrent des femmes avec leurs enfants.

" Regardez cet homme cruel ! " se sont-elles écriées !
" Il marche si rapidement que le pauvre garçon peut à peine le suivre!

Le fermier s'est alors arrêté et a soulevé le garçon pour le mettre derrière lui...

Ils continuèrent leur chemin et étaient presque arrivés au marché quand un commerçant les arrêta .

" Est-ce votre âne? " a-t-il demandé.

" Oui," a répondu le fermier.

" Et bien je suis choqué de la façon dont vous le traitez," a répondu le commerçant.
" Deux personnes à l'arrière d'un âne, c'est trop !
Il va sûrement mourir de cette façon. Vous devriez plutôt le porter!"

Suite à ce conseil, le fermier et son fils descendirent de l'âne, lui lièrent ses pattes et le portèrent. Mais l'âne refusa d'être ainsi porté et, luttant à coups de pattes, il cassa la corde qui tenait ses pattes puis tomba dans une rivière près de la route et se noya. Le fermier n'ayant rien pu faire, il retourna bredouille à la maison.

Someone was observed approached the master and said:

-Excuse me, but you are stubborn! Do you not understand that whenever you attempt to draw the scorpion from water it will sting you?

The master replied:

-The nature of the scorpion is to sting, and it will not change mine who is helping.

Then, using a sheet, the master took the scorpion from the water and saved his life, and continued:

-Do not change your nature if someone hurts you, just take one of cautions in order to prevent pain. In pursuit of happiness, some create it and some wait for it. When life presents you a thousand reasons to cry, show her you thousands reasons to smile ...

Un maître de l'Orient a vu un scorpion se noyer et décida de le tirer de l'eau, et lorsqu'il le fit, le scorpion le piqua. Par l'effet de la douleur le maitre lâcha l'animal qui de nouveau tomba à l'eau en train de se noyer. Le maitre tenta de le tirer de l'eau de nouveau et l'animal le piqua encore.

Quelqu'un était en train d'observer se rapprocha du maitre et lui dit:

-Excusez moi,mais vous êtes têtu ! Ne comprenez vous pas qu'à chaque fois que vous tenteriez de le tirer de l'eau il va vous piquer ?

Le maitre répondit:

-La nature du scorpion est de piquer,et cela ne va pas changer la mienne qui est d'aider.

Alors,à l'aide d'une feuille,le maitre tira le scorpion de l'eau et sauva sa vie et continua :

-Ne change pas ta nature si quelqu'un te fait mal; prends juste des précautions.Les uns poursuivent le bonheur,les autres le créent. Quand la vie te présente mille raisons de pleurer,montre lui que tu as milles raisons pour sourire ...

Perspectives...
==============================


An old man, known for his wisdom, was sitting at the entrance of a city.
A young man approached him and said:
- "I never came here, how are the people who live in this town?"
The old man replied with a question:
- "How were people in the city where you come from?

- "Selfish and evil ... This is also why I was glad to leave," said the young man.
The old man replied: - "You will find the same people here ..."

A little later, another young man approached and asked him exactly the same question:
- "I just arrived in the region, how are the people who live in this town?"
The old man answered again by the same question:
- "Tell me, boy, how were people in the city where you come from?"
- "They were good, friendly and honest, I had good friends and I hated to leave them," replied the young man.
- "You will find the same here," replied the old man.

A merchant who was working nearby had heard both conversations.
As soon as the second young man was gone, he addressed the old man in a tone of reproach:
- "How can you give two contradictory answers to the same question two?
And the old man to answer:
- "Whoever opens his heart also changed his view of the other;
each carries her world in her heart ... "

Un vieil homme, reconnu pour sa grande sagesse, était assis à l'entrée d'une ville.
Un jeune homme s'approcha et lui dit :
- "Je ne suis jamais venu ici ; comment sont les gens qui vivent dans cette ville ?"
Le vieil homme lui répondit par une question :
- "Comment étaient les gens dans la ville d'où tu viens ?

- "Égoïstes et méchants... C'est d'ailleurs la raison pour laquelle j'étais bien content de partir," dit le jeune homme.
Le vieillard répondit : - "Tu trouveras les mêmes gens ici..."

Un peu plus tard, un autre jeune homme s'approcha et lui posa exactement la même question :
- "Je viens d'arriver dans la région ; comment sont les gens qui vivent dans cette ville ?"
Le vieil homme répondit encore par cette même question :
- "Dis-moi, mon garçon, comment étaient les gens dans la ville d'où tu viens ?"
- "Ils étaient bons, accueillants et honnêtes, j'y avais de bons amis et j'ai eu beaucoup de mal à les quitter," répondit le jeune homme.
- "Tu trouveras les mêmes ici", répondit le vieil homme.

Un marchand qui travaillait non loin de là avait entendu les deux conversations.
Dès que le deuxième jeune homme se fut éloigné, il s'adressa au vieillard sur un ton de reproche :
- "Comment pouvez-vous donner deux réponses contradictoires à la même question posée par deux personnes ?
Et le vieillard de lui répondre :
- "Celui qui ouvre son coeur change aussi son regard sur les autres ;
chacun porte son univers dans son coeur..."

--> There was once a man, father of four son.
He wanted to teach his son to 4 not to judge hastily.
Why he sent them to a personal investigation, they had to travel in a remote area to observe a pear tree.
The first son went in winter, the second in spring, the third went in summer and the youngest in the fall.

... When all were gone and came back, he assembled them and asked them to describe what they had seen.
The first son said that the tree was ugly, bent and twisted branches.
The second said no, it was covered with green shoots and full of promise.
The third did not agree and said it was covered with flowers that smelled so good that he thought it was majestic and the most gracious the tree he had ever seen.
The youngest shook his head to emphasize his disapproval by saying that the fruits were all walls, heavy with juice, as a reward.

The good man said to them,
my sons you're all right, you've all been at a different time and therefore have each seen only one season in the life of the tree.

He also told them they could not judge a tree or a human being in just one season and that the essence of what they are really only be judged by only a small part of life, as there are joys, sorrows, regrets ...

If you give up in winter then you will inevitably miss the promise of spring, the beauty of summer and the completion of the fall.

Do not let the pain of one season destroy the joy of all that is yet to come.
Do not judge life on a difficult passage.
Persevere and fear nothing difficult roads,
you can be sure everything changes forever.
